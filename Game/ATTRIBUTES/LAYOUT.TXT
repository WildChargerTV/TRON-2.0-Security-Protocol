/************************************************************************
**
** Screen Layout
**
** The Screen Layout definitions specifies positions and sizes for screen
** components. The GenericScreen section defines default values which will
** be used if the specific screen doesn't override them.
**
** Individual screens may also have custom values specified in their section
** that are used in addition to these common definitions:
**
**	TitlePos (Point)
**
**		The upper-left corner of the title string.
**
**	TitleFont (Integer)
**
**		Specifices which font to use for the title.
**
**	TitleSize (Integer)
**
**		Specifices the size to use for the title.
**
**	PageRect (Rect)
**
**		The rectangle used by the screen items.
**		(left,top,right,bottom)
**
**	ItemSpace (Integer)
**
**		The vertical space between menu items.
**
**	FontFace
**
**		Specifices which font to use by default for items.
**
**	FontSize
**
**		Specifices default size at which to render text.
**
**	MusicIntensity
**
**		Specifies what the menu intensity should be for this
**		screen (1-7, with 1=silence, 7=high action)
**
** Typical Custom Components:
**-------------------------------------
**	FX#
**		Plays an FX registered in the FX Layout Area of this
**		text file  upon the loading of a screen.  You can call
**		up any number of FX as long as they are sequentially
**		numbered. i.e.
**		FX0 	= "happyfx"
**		FX1 	= "notSoHappyFX"
**
**	IntroFX#
**		This will call up a registered FX at the start of a screen.
**		When this FX is done the LoopFX with the same number will be
**		launched.  i.e.
**		IntroFX0...........LoopFX0
**		IntroFX1.....LoopFX1
**
**	ShortIntroFX#
**		This FX will be called up as an alternate to IntroFX for
**		menus that will be seen often.
**
**	LoopFX#
**		LoopFX are initiated after the IntroFX or ShortIntroFX and
**		are terminated as soon as you switch menus.
**
**	ScaleName#
**		This calls up a ScaleFX from FX.txt rather than an FX from FXEd.
**
**	Light#
**		Lights described in the Lights Layouts area are called up by this
**		flag.
**
*/

/************************************************************************
**
**	HUD Layout
**
** The HUDLayout definitions specify positions and sizes for in-game
** interface components. If an attribute isn't listed for a particular
** layout, the base value defined in the default layout (*)()HUDLayout0(*)() will
** be used.
**
** Note:
**		Positions (AmmoBasePos, etc.) are relative to a 640x480 resolution
**			and will be scaled for other resolutions.
**		Offsets are absolute distances and are not scaled for different
**			resolutions.
**
**
**	LayoutName (Integer)
**
**		The string id used to identify the layout configuration.
**
**	BarHeight (Integer)
**
**		The height used for the HUD bars.
**
**	BarScale (Float)
**
**		The number of pixels per point in the various HUD bars.
**
**
**	AmmoBasePos (Point)
**
**		Relative position of the Ammo display.
**
**	UseAmmoBar (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides ammo bar, 1 - shows ammo bar
**
**	AmmoClipOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-RIGHT corner of the ammo-in-clip bar.
**
**	AmmoBarOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-RIGHT corner of the total ammo bar.
**
**	UseAmmoText (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides ammo text, 1 - shows ammo text
**
**	AmmoTextOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the ammo text.
**
**	AmmoIconOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the ammo type icon.
**
**
**	HealthBasePos (Point)
**
**		Relative position of the Health and Armor display.
**
**	UseHealthBar (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides health bar, 1 - shows health bar
**
**	HealthBarOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the health bar.
**
**	ArmorBarOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the  upper-left corner of the armor bar.
**
**	UseHealthText (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides health/armor text, 1 - shows health/armor text
**
**	HealthTextOffset (Point)
**
**		Text is justified to right so that the x coordinate is the offset of the right edge
**		of the text from the center of the screen.
**		The y coordinate is the offset of the center of the text from the
**		bottom of the screen.
**
**	EnergyTextOffset (Point)
**
**		Text is justified to left so that the x coordinate is the offset of the left edge
**		of the text from the center of the screen.
**		The y coordinate is the offset of the center of the text from the
**		bottom of the screen.
**
**	UseHealthIcon (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides health/armor icons, 1 - shows health/armor icons
**
**	HealthIconOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the health icon.
**
**	ArmorIconOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the armor icon.
**
**
**
**	AirBasePos (Point)
**
**		Relative position of the Air display.
**
**	UseAirIcon (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides air icon, 1 - shows air icon
**
**	AirIconOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the air icon.
**
**	UseAirText (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides air text, 1 - shows air text
**
**	AirTextOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the air text.
**
**	UseAirBar (Integer)
**
**		0 - hides air bar, 1 - shows air bar
**
**	AirBarOffset (Point)
**
**		Offset to the upper-left corner of the air bar.
**
**	ModeTextPos (Point)
**
**		Relative position of the sunglass mode text display.
**
**
**
**	DamageBasePos (Point)
**
**		Relative position (upper-left corner) of the damage display.
**
**
**	HealthEnergyFont (integer)
**		Index number for the font to use on the health/energy indicators
**
**	InfoTextPos
**	InfoTextSize
**	InfoTextWidth
**	InfoTextJustify
**	InfoTextAlpha
**	InfoTextColor
**	InfoTextBadAlpha
**	InfoTextBadColor
**	ProfilerPos (Point)
**		Location for top-left corner of Profiler data spew
*/

**
**	Menu Piece Layout  (Formerly known as Character Layout.)
**
**
**	Name	(String)
**
**		Programmer friendly name for the menu piece.
**
**	Model (String)
**
**		Path to the Model Resource.
**
**	Skin# (String)
**
**		Path to the Skin Resource.  For multiple skins use like Skin0, Skin1...
**
**	RenderStyle (String)
**
**		Path to the RenderStyle Resource.  For multiple renderstyles use like
**		RenderStyle0, Renderstyle1...
**
**	Pos (Vector)
**
**		Position at which piece should be placed.  Items that have a ParentPiece declared
**		Should not have a Pos Attribute.
**
**	IntroAnim (String)
**
**		Name of the Intro Animation to play.
**		If there is an Intro Animation the code attempts to play that animation and then
**		play the loop animation.  If there is a LoopAnim Specified the code will attempt to
**		play that particular animation rather than the animation called "loop".
**
**	LoopAnim (String)
**
**		Name of the Loop Animation to play.
**		If there is no LoopAnim Attribute, than the code looks for an animation called "loop".
**		If there is no LoopAnim declared, and no animation in the model called "loop", then
**		the first animation in the list gets played.
**		If you want a particular animation played as the loop use this field to declare just which
**		animation that is and all sorts of happiness will come to you.
**
**	ParentPiece (String)
**
**	The Model to which this model will attach itself to.
**
**	ParentSocket (String)
**
**		The Socket to which this model's position will be moved.
**
**	Layer (int)
**
**		The layer on which this model exists for rendering order purposes.
**		Lower numbers are rendered first and behind whatever numbers come after,
**		regardless of actual position in the world.  A sort of Visual Caste System ya know...
**
*/

/************************************************************************
**
**	Screen Transition Declarations
**
**	A Transition will override the FX associated with a single menu screen.
**	So when a screen gains focus, it will first inquire of the
**	TransitionFXMgr if it has a special set of FX for the intro. If there
**	is one, then BaseScreen will use that one.	If not, then BaseScreen
**	will use the Intro/ShortIntro/LoopFX combo --if any-- for that screen.
**
**	In a similar vein, if a Transition has a "From" field but no "To" field,
**	then this transition will always be used when leaving the "From" screen.
**	If a Transition has a "To" field but no "From field, then it will be the
**	equivilant to the IntroFX baked into that particular screen.  The rules
**	of precedence will be that Transitions will always override something
**	baked into a  screen's layout, and a Transition with both a "From" and a
**	"To" field will always have precedence over a Transitions with just one
**	of these two fields.
**
**	notes:
**	*LoopFX will be played after the IntroFX with the corresponding number
**	has ended.	For example when IntroFX1 finishes LoopFX1 should launch if
**	indeed there actually was one.
**	*If there are any duplicate Transitions only the first one will be used.
**	*The FX being called must first be defined in the FX Layout area of this
**	file.
**
**	(*)()Transition0(*)()
**	TransitionFrom			= "ScreenSingle"
**	TransitionTo			= "ScreenMain"
**	IntroFX0				= "Menu_Main_Intro"
**	ShortIntroFX0			= "Menu_Main_Introsh"
**	LoopFX0 				= "Menu_Main_Loop"
**	IntroFX1				= "AuxiliaryFX1"
**
**/
